Generate a practical compliance checklist for the activity, regulation, or topic described in: $ARGUMENTS

## Your Task

Identify the relevant Canadian regulatory frameworks based on the topic and create an actionable checklist tailored to a business/operations team.

## Checklist Format

### [Topic] — Canadian Compliance Checklist

**Applicable Regulations:** [List the specific Acts and regulations that apply]
**Jurisdiction:** [Federal / Province(s)]
**Last reviewed against:** [Legislation version / year]

---

For each regulatory area, produce a checklist section with checkbox items. Group items logically. Each item must be:
- **Actionable** — a specific task someone can do and check off, not a vague principle
- **Referenced** — cite the specific Act section or regulatory requirement in brackets, e.g. `[PIPEDA s.4.3]`
- **Plain language** — understandable to a non-lawyer

**Example section format:**

#### Privacy (PIPEDA)
- [ ] Appoint a Privacy Officer and document their responsibilities `[PIPEDA Principle 1]`
- [ ] Document the purpose for each category of personal information collected before collection begins `[PIPEDA Principle 2]`
- [ ] Ensure privacy policy is publicly available on the website `[PIPEDA Principle 8]`

---

After the checklist, include:

### Quick-Start Priorities
The 3 most important items to address first if the organization is starting from scratch, and why.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
2–3 frequent compliance errors organizations make in this area.

### Where to Get Help
Point to the relevant Canadian regulator(s) and any free government resources.

---

Calibrate depth to the topic: narrow topics (e.g. "WHMIS labelling") get a detailed checklist; broad topics (e.g. "opening a new office in BC") get a high-level checklist with pointers to more detailed resources.
