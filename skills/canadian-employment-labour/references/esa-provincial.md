# ESA Provincial Deep Reference: Overtime, Records & Vacation

## Overtime Pay Comparison

| Jurisdiction | Overtime Threshold | Overtime Rate |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Federal (CLC) | After 8 hrs/day or 40 hrs/week | 1.5× regular rate |
| Ontario (ESA) | After 44 hrs/week | 1.5× regular rate |
| BC (BCESA) | After 8 hrs/day or 40 hrs/week; after 12 hrs/day | 1.5× / 2× after 12 hrs |
| Alberta (ESC) | After 8 hrs/day or 44 hrs/week | 1.5× regular rate |

**Overtime averaging agreements:** Ontario and Alberta permit overtime averaging agreements with employee consent. Employers must file these and follow prescribed formats.

## Vacation Entitlement

| Jurisdiction | Year 1–4 | Year 5+ | Pay Rate |
|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| Federal | 2 weeks | 3 weeks (yr 6+: 4 weeks yr 8+) | 4% / 6% / 8% of wages |
| Ontario | 2 weeks | 3 weeks (5+ yrs) | 4% / 6% |
| BC | 2 weeks | 3 weeks (5+ yrs) | 4% / 6% |
| Alberta | 2 weeks | 3 weeks (5+ yrs) | 4% / 6% |

## Statutory Holidays

### Ontario (9 public holidays)
New Year's Day, Family Day, Good Friday, Victoria Day, Canada Day, Labour Day, Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day, Boxing Day.

**Public holiday pay formula:** Total regular wages + vacation pay earned in 4 work weeks before PH ÷ 20.

### BC (10 public holidays)
Same as Ontario plus BC Day and Remembrance Day (November 11).

### Alberta (9 public holidays)
New Year's Day, Alberta Family Day, Good Friday, Victoria Day, Canada Day, Labour Day, Thanksgiving Day, Remembrance Day, Christmas Day.

**Note:** In Alberta, employees must work the shift before and after the statutory holiday to qualify for stat pay (with limited exceptions).

### Federal (11 public holidays)
Same as national list plus Federal Day (varies).

## Record-Keeping Obligations

### Ontario (ESA, O. Reg. 285/01)
Employers must retain:
- Hours worked each day and week for 3 years
- Wages paid for 3 years
- Vacation pay accrual records for 3 years
- Leaves of absence records
- Written agreements (overtime, excess hours) for duration of employment + 3 years

### BC (BCESA)
- Payroll records for 4 years
- Time records showing start/end and breaks for hourly employees

### Alberta (ESC)
- Payroll and time records for 3 years after end of employment
- Records available for inspection at any time by Employment Standards Officer

## Independent Contractor vs. Employee

The distinction matters for all employment standards obligations. Canadian courts apply a multi-factor test (no single factor is determinative):

| Factor | Points to Employment | Points to Contractor |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Control | Employer controls how work is done | Worker controls method |
| Ownership of tools | Employer provides tools | Worker owns tools |
| Chance of profit / risk of loss | Worker has no financial risk | Worker bears financial risk |
| Integration | Work integral to employer's business | Work is accessory |
| Exclusivity | Worker works only for one client | Worker has multiple clients |
| Written agreement | Employment agreement | Contractor agreement |

**Important:** A contract calling someone an "independent contractor" does not make them one. If the economic reality is employment, ESA obligations apply. The Ontario ESA includes a **reverse onus**: employer must prove contractor status if challenged.

## Progressive Discipline Best Practices

Document all disciplinary steps to support a just cause defense:
1. Verbal warning (documented by manager)
2. Written warning (signed by employee)
3. Final written warning / suspension without pay
4. Termination for cause (with full documentation of all prior steps)

**Statute of limitations on misconduct:** Old misconduct that was condoned (employer knew and took no action) generally cannot support just cause termination. Act promptly on discovered misconduct.
