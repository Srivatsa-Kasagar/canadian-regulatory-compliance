# Canadian Employment & Labour Compliance

## Jurisdiction Map

The first question in any employment matter is: **which law applies?**

| Employer Type | Governing Legislation |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Federally regulated (banks, telecoms, airlines, interprovincial transport, broadcasting) | Canada Labour Code (CLC) |
| Ontario-based employer | Employment Standards Act, 2000 (ESA ON) |
| BC-based employer | Employment Standards Act BC (BCESA) |
| Alberta-based employer | Employment Standards Code AB (ESC AB) |

Federally regulated employers comprise roughly **6% of the Canadian workforce**. When uncertain, default to provincial law.

## Termination: Notice and Severance Requirements

### Federal (Canada Labour Code)
- **Notice:** 2 weeks (employees with 3+ months service); more if employment contract provides.
- **Unjust dismissal:** Employees with 12+ months service and not covered by a collective agreement may file an unjust dismissal complaint (s.240 CLC). Remedy can include reinstatement.
- **Group termination (50+ employees):** 16 weeks' notice to Minister of Employment; individual notice still required.

### Ontario (ESA, 2000 — Parts XV and XVI)
- **Individual notice (s.57):** Based on length of service:

| Years of Service | Minimum Notice |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Less than 1 year | 1 week |
| 1–3 years | 2 weeks |
| 3–4 years | 3 weeks |
| 4–5 years | 4 weeks |
| 5–6 years | 5 weeks |
| 6–7 years | 6 weeks |
| 7–8 years | 7 weeks |
| 8+ years | 8 weeks |

- **Severance pay (s.64):** Applies if: employer has global payroll ≥ $2.5M AND employee has 5+ years service. Calculation: 1 week per year of service (max 26 weeks), applied to regular wages.
- **Termination pay in lieu:** Employer may pay wages instead of working notice.
- **Mass termination (50+ employees within 4 weeks):** Enhanced notice requirements apply (8–16 weeks depending on number affected); notice to Director of Employment Standards required.

### BC (Employment Standards Act BC)
- **Notice:** Based on service:

| Years of Service | Minimum Notice |
|-----------------|---------------|
| 3–12 months | 1 week |
| 1–3 years | 2 weeks |
| 3–4 years | 3 weeks |
| 4–5 years | 4 weeks |
| 5–6 years | 5 weeks |
| 6–7 years | 6 weeks |
| 7–8 years | 7 weeks |
| 8+ years | 8 weeks |

- **Group termination (50+ in 2 months):** 8–16 weeks' notice depending on number; notice to Employment Standards Branch required.

### Alberta (Employment Standards Code)
- **Notice:** Based on service:

| Years of Service | Minimum Notice |
|-----------------|---------------|
| 90 days – 2 years | 1 week |
| 2–4 years | 2 weeks |
| 4–6 years | 4 weeks |
| 6–8 years | 5 weeks |
| 8–10 years | 6 weeks |
| 10+ years | 8 weeks |

- **No statutory severance** pay in Alberta (unlike Ontario).

### Just Cause Dismissal
Statutory minimum notice is **not required** if the dismissal is for just cause. Just cause is a high bar — typically includes serious misconduct, theft, fraud, persistent insubordination, or repeated performance issues despite progressive discipline. Document everything. When in doubt, provide notice or pay in lieu to reduce wrongful dismissal risk.

## Human Rights — Canadian Human Rights Act (Federal) & Provincial Codes

### Protected Grounds
Under the CHRA (federal) and provincial equivalents, employers cannot discriminate based on:
Race, national/ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity/expression, marital status, family status, disability, a conviction for which a pardon has been granted.

Provincial codes add grounds (e.g., Ontario adds: ancestry, place of origin, creed, receipt of public assistance, record of offences).

### Duty to Accommodate
Employers have a **duty to accommodate** employees with a disability (or other protected ground) to the point of **undue hardship**. Undue hardship is assessed considering: cost, health and safety risks, and (for larger employers) the financial ability to bear costs.

Steps:
1. Employee discloses a need for accommodation.
2. Employer requests sufficient medical/supporting documentation.
3. Parties collaborate to identify workable accommodation options.
4. Employer implements the solution unless undue hardship is demonstrated.
5. Document all steps and decisions.

### Harassment
Employers have an obligation to maintain a harassment-free workplace. Federal employers (CLC Part II) must have a workplace harassment and violence prevention policy. Ontario employers (OHSA) must have a workplace harassment policy and annual review. Failure to investigate complaints promptly is itself a violation.

## Leaves of Absence

### Federal (Canada Labour Code)
| Leave | Duration | Key Rule |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Maternity | 17 weeks | Unpaid; job-protected |
| Parental | 63 weeks (standard) / 71 weeks (extended) | Unpaid; job-protected |
| Personal/sick | 5 days (3 paid after 3 months) | |
| Bereavement | 10 days (3 paid) | |
| Family violence | 10 days (5 paid) | |

### Ontario (ESA, 2000)
| Leave | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Pregnancy | Up to 17 weeks |
| Parental | Up to 61 weeks (non-birth parent); 63 weeks (birth parent) |
| Family medical | Up to 28 weeks in 52-week period |
| Sick leave | 3 days unpaid |
| Bereavement | 2 days unpaid |
| Domestic or sexual violence | Up to 10 days + 15 weeks |

## Pay Equity

**Federal Pay Equity Act (in force 2021):** Proactive regime for federally regulated employers with 10+ employees. Employers must establish and maintain a pay equity plan, identify wage gaps, and close them over time. Pay equity committees required for 100+ employees.

**Ontario Pay Equity Act:** Requires equal pay for work of equal value in public and broader public sector; private sector employers with 10+ employees must also comply.

**Quebec Pay Equity Act:** Applies to employers with 10+ employees; proactive obligation to establish and maintain pay equity.
