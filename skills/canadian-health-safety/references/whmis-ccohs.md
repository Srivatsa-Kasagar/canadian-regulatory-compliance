# WHMIS & Incident Investigation Deep Reference

## WHMIS 2015 Label Review Checklist

Use this checklist to audit supplier and workplace labels:

**Supplier Label — Required Elements**
- [ ] Product identifier (name and/or number that uniquely identifies the product)
- [ ] Name, address, and telephone number of Canadian supplier
- [ ] At least one hazard pictogram (where applicable)
- [ ] Signal word ("Danger" or "Warning" — not both; if multiple hazards, use the more severe)
- [ ] Hazard statement(s) corresponding to assigned hazard classes/categories
- [ ] Precautionary statement(s) — prevention, response, storage, disposal
- [ ] SDS availability statement (if label space is limited)
- [ ] Supplemental label elements (trade name, product use, directions, etc. as required)
- [ ] Language: English and French for federally regulated workplaces; English in common law provinces (French translation recommended for bilingual workplaces)

**Workplace Label — Required Elements** (for decanted/bulk containers)
- [ ] Product identifier matching the supplier label / SDS
- [ ] Safe handling information (key hazards and PPE required)
- [ ] Reference to SDS: "See Safety Data Sheet" or equivalent

**Red Flags During Label Review**
- Signal word "Danger" and "Warning" both appearing — only one is correct
- Hazard pictogram present with no corresponding hazard statement
- Missing Canadian supplier contact information
- SDS revision date more than 3 years old (best practice; not legally mandated in all cases but indicates outdated hazard information)
- Product listed as WHMIS-controlled but no label affixed

## SDS Section-by-Section Audit Tool

| Section | Key Items to Verify |
|---------|-------------------|
| 1 – Identification | Product name matches label; supplier info complete; recommended use stated |
| 2 – Hazard Identification | All relevant GHS hazard classes listed; signal word and pictograms consistent with label |
| 3 – Composition | CAS numbers for all hazardous ingredients; concentrations or concentration ranges |
| 4 – First Aid | Routes of exposure covered; symptoms described; when to seek medical attention |
| 5 – Fire-Fighting | Suitable extinguishing media; unsuitable media; hazardous combustion products |
| 6 – Accidental Release | Spill containment; PPE for cleanup; disposal guidance |
| 7 – Handling and Storage | Incompatibilities; storage temperature/conditions; ventilation requirements |
| 8 – Exposure Controls / PPE | OELs/TLVs listed; specific PPE (gloves, respirator type, eye protection) specified |
| 9 – Physical/Chemical | Physical state, pH, flash point, boiling point — all relevant fields populated |
| 11 – Toxicological | Acute and chronic health effects; routes of exposure (inhalation, dermal, ingestion) |
| 16 – Other Information | Preparation and last-revision date present |

## Incident Investigation Template

### Section 1: Incident Details
- Date and time of incident
- Location (specific area/workstation/address)
- Type of incident: Injury / Near-miss / Property damage / Occupational illness / Dangerous occurrence
- Was work stopped? Yes / No

### Section 2: People Involved
- Injured/affected worker(s): Name, job title, tenure, shift
- Witnesses: Names and contact information
- Supervisors on duty at time of incident

### Section 3: Description of Incident
(Narrative: What happened, in chronological order, from start of shift to incident. Be factual; avoid assigning blame.)

### Section 4: Immediate Causes
**Unsafe Acts (worker behaviour):**
- Operating without authority
- Failure to warn or secure
- Operating at improper speed
- Using defective equipment
- Failure to use PPE
- Horseplay / distraction

**Unsafe Conditions (workplace/environment):**
- Inadequate guarding or barriers
- Defective tools or equipment
- Inadequate lighting or ventilation
- Poor housekeeping
- Hazardous environmental condition

### Section 5: Root Causes (Contributing Factors)
- Training deficiency: Was worker trained? Was training adequate? Was training current?
- Supervision deficiency: Was task supervised? Were hazards identified?
- Procedure deficiency: Was there a written procedure? Was it followed?
- Engineering/design deficiency: Was the workstation/equipment designed safely?
- System/management deficiency: Were hazards identified in a prior risk assessment?

## Ontario OHSA — Forms Reference

- **Form 7 (WSIB):** Report of Injury/Disease — submit within 3 business days of learning of injury requiring healthcare beyond first aid
- **Ministry of Labour notice:** Telephone immediately for critical injury/fatality; written notice within 48 hours
- **JHSC investigation:** Legally required for critical injuries; written report to be prepared

## WorkSafeBC Reporting Thresholds

| Event | Action Required |
|-------|----------------|
| Worker fatality | Immediate phone notification to WorkSafeBC |
| Serious injury (permanent disability, hospitalization >2 days) | Immediate notification |
| Incident with serious potential (near-miss with high potential for serious injury) | Notify within 48 hours |
| Minor injury (first aid only) | Record in first aid register; no mandatory report |
| Occupational disease | Report when diagnosed |
