# Canadian Immigration Deep Reference

## LMIA Application Checklist

### Before Applying
- [ ] Confirm position requires an LMIA (not LMIA-exempt under IMP)
- [ ] Verify occupation NOC code (National Occupational Classification)
- [ ] Determine wage: compare to provincial/territorial median wage to identify high-wage vs. low-wage stream
- [ ] Confirm employer is in good standing (no outstanding compliance violations)
- [ ] Prepare $1,000 processing fee per position (waived for some agricultural streams)

### Recruitment (must be completed before applying)
- [ ] Post position on **Job Bank** (canada.ca/jobbank) for minimum **4 weeks**
- [ ] Use at least **2 additional recruitment methods** appropriate to the occupation:
  - General job boards (LinkedIn, Indeed, Workopolis, Monster)
  - Industry/professional associations
  - Post-secondary institution career centres (for recent graduates)
  - Indigenous employment centres (for positions in some regions)
  - International recruitment (only after Canadian efforts exhausted)
- [ ] Document all recruitment activities: dates posted, platforms used, number of applicants, reasons for non-selection of Canadian applicants
- [ ] Retain all recruitment records for **6 years**

### Application Package
- [ ] Completed LMIA application form (EMP5593 for most streams)
- [ ] Job description (duties, requirements, wages, hours, benefits)
- [ ] Proof of recruitment efforts (screenshots, job postings, correspondence)
- [ ] Summary of Canadian/PR applicants reviewed and reasons for non-hire
- [ ] Transition plan (high-wage stream only)
- [ ] Business legitimacy documents (CRA business number, incorporation docs, financial statements if requested)
- [ ] Housing plan/documentation (agricultural streams)

### After Receiving Positive LMIA
- [ ] Provide foreign national with copy of LMIA confirmation letter (they need it to apply for work permit)
- [ ] Retain copy of LMIA and all supporting documents for 6 years
- [ ] Ensure work permit conditions match LMIA (occupation, wage, location, duration)

## CUSMA/USMCA Professional Categories (Selected)

US and Mexican citizens in the following occupations may work in Canada without an LMIA under CUSMA:

| Category | Minimum Credentials |
|----------|-------------------|
| Accountant | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree; or CPA, CA, CGA, or CMA |
| Computer Systems Analyst | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree; or Post-Secondary Diploma/Certificate + 3 years experience |
| Engineer | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree; or State/Provincial licence |
| Lawyer (including Notary in Quebec) | LL.B., J.D., LL.L., B.C.L., or Licenciatura degree; or membership in a provincial bar |
| Management Consultant | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree; or equivalent professional experience (5 years in consulting) |
| Scientist (various) | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree |
| Graphic Designer | Baccalaureate or Licenciatura degree; or Post-Secondary Diploma/Certificate + 3 years experience |
| Medical/Allied Professionals | Various (physician, dentist, pharmacist, etc.) — degree + licence |

**Application process:** Applicant applies at port of entry (US/Mexico citizens) or at a Canadian visa office. Employer provides offer of employment letter; no IRCC portal submission required for CUSMA.

## IMP — Offer of Employment Steps

1. **Register** employer in the IRCC Employer Portal (one-time)
2. **Submit** an Offer of Employment for each foreign national — include:
   - Position details (NOC code, job title, duties, wage, hours)
   - Employment terms (start date, duration, location)
   - LMIA exemption code (select the applicable IMP category)
3. **Pay** the $230 Employer Compliance Fee
4. **Receive** the Offer of Employment number
5. **Provide** the number to the foreign national — they include it in their work permit application
6. **Retain** all documentation for 6 years

## Employer Inspection Preparation Guide

ESDC inspections can occur at any time within 6 years of hiring a TFW. Prepare a compliance binder:

### Records to Maintain
| Document | Retention Period |
|---------|----------------|
| LMIA and supporting documents | 6 years |
| Employment contracts (signed) | 6 years |
| Payroll records (pay stubs, T4s) | 6 years |
| Time and attendance records | 6 years |
| Housing documentation (if applicable) | 6 years |
| Health insurance records | 6 years |
| Transportation receipts (TFWP) | 6 years |
| Recruitment records | 6 years |

### Common Inspection Findings (Non-Compliance)
- Paying less than the committed wage (even by a small amount)
- Changing the worker's duties without amending the work permit
- Failing to provide health insurance during the waiting period
- Inadequate housing (agricultural workers)
- Retaining the worker's passport or documents (prohibited)
- Charging the worker for LMIA costs (prohibited — must be borne by employer)

### If Selected for Inspection
1. Notify HR and legal counsel immediately
2. Gather all records from the compliance binder
3. Be cooperative — obstruction worsens outcomes
4. Verify payroll records match committed wages for every pay period
5. Document any legitimate discrepancies proactively

## Work Permit Duration Guidelines

| Permit Type | Typical Duration |
|-------------|----------------|
| LMIA-based closed permit | Up to 2 years (tied to LMIA validity) |
| CUSMA/USMCA | 1 year, renewable |
| ICT (Executive/Senior Manager) | Up to 3 years (7 years max cumulative) |
| ICT (Specialized Knowledge) | Up to 3 years (5 years max cumulative) |
| Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) | Equal to length of study (max 3 years) |
| Open Work Permit (spouse) | Tied to principal applicant's permit |

**Renewal:** Apply for renewal at least **30 days before expiry** to maintain legal status under "maintained status" rules.
