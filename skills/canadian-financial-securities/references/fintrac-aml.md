# FINTRAC AML Deep Reference

## Red Flags by Sector

### All Reporting Entities
- Client declines to provide required identification or provides false/inconsistent information
- Client appears nervous, evasive, or reluctant to answer routine questions
- Transactions structured just below $10,000 threshold (structuring)
- Unusual interest in reporting or record-keeping requirements
- Transaction inconsistent with client's known business, occupation, or income
- Use of nominees or third parties without clear legitimate reason
- Client's address is a mail drop, P.O. box, or unknown location

### Real Estate
- All-cash purchase with no apparent source of funds
- Property purchased then quickly resold at a loss (round-tripping)
- Multiple properties purchased simultaneously by related parties
- Client insists on no legal representation
- Transaction price significantly above or below market value

### Virtual Currency / Cryptocurrency
- Multiple small cryptocurrency transactions aggregated to stay below thresholds
- Transactions routed through multiple wallets to obscure trail
- Use of mixing/tumbling services
- Purchase of cryptocurrency followed by immediate transfer to unknown wallet
- Peer-to-peer transactions with no KYC

### Financial Entities / Securities
- Rapid movement of funds between unrelated accounts
- Investments that don't match client's risk profile or stated goals
- Wire transfers to/from high-risk jurisdictions with no business purpose
- Client withdraws large amounts immediately after deposit (in-out transactions)
- Dormant accounts suddenly showing large activity

## Compliance Program Template

### Required Elements (FINTRAC Guidelines)

**1. Written Policies and Procedures**
- Client identification and verification procedures
- Ongoing monitoring procedures
- PEP/HIO screening and enhanced due diligence procedures
- Transaction monitoring and reporting procedures
- Record-keeping procedures
- Training program description

**2. Risk Assessment**
Document an organizational risk assessment covering:
- Client/customer risks (geographic, occupation, transaction types)
- Product/service risks
- Geographic risks (countries transacted with)
- Distribution channel risks

Frequency: Update at minimum every 2 years, or whenever a significant change occurs.

**3. Compliance Officer**
- Must be a senior employee with authority to implement the program
- Responsibilities: policies, training, reporting, FINTRAC liaison
- Designate a backup/alternate

**4. Training**
- All relevant employees trained at hire and annually thereafter
- Training must cover: PCMLTFA obligations, client identification, red flags, reporting, penalties
- Keep records of training dates and attendees

**5. Independent Effectiveness Review**
- Review every 2 years (or more frequently based on risk)
- Conducted by someone independent of the compliance function
- Must assess all 5 elements of the compliance program
- Findings documented; corrective actions tracked

## STR Writing Guidance

When drafting or documenting a Suspicious Transaction Report:

**Required fields:**
- Reporting entity information
- Transaction details (date, amount, currency, account numbers)
- Subject information (client identification details collected)
- Grounds for suspicion (detailed narrative)

**Narrative best practices:**
1. State the transaction(s) that triggered the review
2. Describe the client's known profile and how it compares
3. List specific red flags observed
4. Describe any explanation offered by the client and why it was or was not satisfactory
5. Conclude with the basis for reasonable grounds to suspect

**Example narrative structure:**
> "On [date], the reporting entity received [amount] from [client] via [method]. This transaction is inconsistent with the client's stated occupation as [occupation] and established transaction profile, which consists of [normal activity]. Specifically, [red flag 1], [red flag 2], and [red flag 3] were observed. When asked about the source of funds, the client stated [response], which was not consistent with [reason]. Accordingly, there are reasonable grounds to suspect this transaction may be related to money laundering."

## FINTRAC Penalties (Administrative Monetary Penalties — AMPs)

| Violation Level | Per Violation | Annual Cap |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Minor | Up to $1,000 | $100,000 |
| Serious | Up to $100,000 | $500,000 |
| Very Serious | Up to $500,000 | $1,000,000 |
| Criminal | Up to $2,000,000 / 5 years imprisonment | N/A |

Penalties are publicly disclosed. Since 2008, FINTRAC has published the names of penalized entities.

## High-Risk Countries (Reference)

FINTRAC and FATF designate certain jurisdictions as high-risk or subject to enhanced monitoring. As of 2025, FATF's high-risk / other monitored jurisdictions include: Iran, North Korea, Myanmar, and countries on the grey list (subject to increased monitoring). Always verify the current FATF list at fatf-gafi.org before assessing geographic risk.
