# FCAC Consumer Protection Deep Reference

## Complaint Handling Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist to assess whether your bank's complaint handling process meets FCAC requirements:

### Written Policy
- [ ] Written complaint handling policy published on website and available in branch
- [ ] Policy covers all products and services offered to retail customers
- [ ] Policy available in English and French
- [ ] Policy reviewed and updated at least annually

### Intake and Acknowledgement
- [ ] Multiple channels for submitting complaints: in person, phone, mail, online
- [ ] Dedicated email address or online form for complaints
- [ ] Written acknowledgement sent within 5 business days of receiving complaint
- [ ] Acknowledgement includes: reference number, name of contact person, expected timeline

### Investigation and Response
- [ ] Final written response provided within 56 calendar days
- [ ] Response clearly explains: decision, reasons, and any remedies offered
- [ ] If complaint cannot be resolved within 56 days, written update provided explaining delay
- [ ] Complaint records retained for minimum 3 years

### Referral to External Body
- [ ] Final response includes name and contact information of the ECB (OBSI or ADRBO)
- [ ] Customer informed of their right to escalate to the ECB at no cost
- [ ] Bank is registered member in good standing with its designated ECB
- [ ] Annual complaints data reported to FCAC

### Prohibited Practices
- [ ] No fee charged to customers for submitting a complaint
- [ ] No requirement for customers to waive rights as a condition of complaint resolution
- [ ] No retaliation against customers who file complaints (e.g., account closure without valid reason)

## Credit Card Disclosure Template Review

When reviewing a credit card agreement or disclosure document, check for these required elements:

**Key Disclosure Box (must appear prominently at the front):**
- [ ] Annual interest rate — purchases
- [ ] Annual interest rate — cash advances (often higher)
- [ ] Annual interest rate — balance transfers
- [ ] Annual fee (if any)
- [ ] Grace period (minimum 21 days)
- [ ] Minimum payment formula (e.g., greater of $10 or 2% of balance)

**Statement Requirements:**
- [ ] Minimum payment amount shown
- [ ] Payment due date shown
- [ ] "Minimum payment warning" box: time to pay off balance at minimum payments and total interest cost
- [ ] Interest charges broken out by transaction type
- [ ] Credit limit and available credit shown

**Common Deficiencies Found by FCAC:**
- Interest rate on cash advances not disclosed separately from purchases
- Grace period disclosed as "up to 21 days" rather than exact number of days
- Minimum payment warning missing or incomplete
- Balance protection insurance not clearly identified as optional
- Annual fee buried in fine print rather than in key disclosure box

## Prepayment Penalty Worked Examples

### Example 1 — IRD Penalty (Fixed Rate, Rates Have Fallen)

**Scenario:**
- Original mortgage: $500,000 at 5.89% fixed, 5-year term
- Breaking mortgage with 2.5 years remaining
- Current posted rate for 2.5-year term: 4.19%

**Calculation:**
- IRD rate = 5.89% − 4.19% = 1.70%
- Remaining principal: $462,000 (after payments)
- Penalty = $462,000 × 1.70% × (30 months ÷ 12) = **$19,635**

**3 months' interest alternative:**
- $462,000 × 5.89% × (3 ÷ 12) = $6,802

**Penalty = higher of IRD vs. 3 months' interest = $19,635**

### Example 2 — IRD Penalty (Rates Have Risen — IRD May Be Lower)

**Scenario:**
- Same mortgage at 5.89%, 2.5 years remaining
- Current posted rate for 2.5-year term: 6.50% (rates have risen)

**Calculation:**
- IRD rate = 5.89% − 6.50% = **negative** → IRD = $0 (no differential to charge)

**Penalty = higher of $0 vs. 3 months' interest = $6,802**

**Key insight:** When rates rise after you take out a mortgage, you typically pay only 3 months' interest to break it. When rates fall, the IRD can be very significant.

## NSF Fee Compliance Guide

### FCAC Voluntary Code Commitments (Major Banks, 2023)
Following FCAC pressure, Canada's major banks committed to:
- Cap NSF fees at **$25 per item** (reduced from $45–$48 previously)
- Provide at least **12 hours' notice** before charging an NSF fee (giving customers time to cover the shortfall)
- Allow at least **one free NSF event per year** for customers in good standing (some banks)

### Bank Obligations
- Disclose NSF fee in the fee disclosure document provided at account opening
- Disclose NSF fee in annual fee disclosure (statement insert or online)
- Provide 30 days' written notice before increasing the NSF fee
- NSF fee must be reasonable and proportionate — FCAC monitors for excessive fees

### Customer Rights
- Right to receive advance notice (12 hours) before NSF fee is applied (where bank has committed to this)
- Right to receive clear information about which transaction triggered the NSF
- Right to complain to FCAC if the bank's NSF fee practices are unfair or undisclosed

## Right to Basic Banking — Refusal Scenarios

Banks frequently struggle with the line between legitimate refusal grounds and discrimination. FCAC guidance clarifies:

**Legitimate refusal grounds:**
- Criminal record for financial crime (fraud, theft, forgery) in the past **7 years** — bank must be able to show the record
- Individual has made a false statement to the bank in connection with the account request
- Bank has reasonable grounds to believe the account will be used for illegal purposes
- Individual has previously committed fraud or financial crime against this specific bank

**NOT legitimate refusal grounds (discrimination risk):**
- No fixed address (homeless individuals have a right to a basic account)
- Receiving social assistance
- Low credit score or prior bankruptcies
- No credit history
- Non-citizen or non-permanent resident status (if valid ID is provided)
- The individual appears to be from a particular racial or ethnic background

**Required ID:** Bank must accept any two of: passport, driver's licence, provincial health card, Indian status card, permanent resident card, government-issued photo ID. Bank cannot require a social insurance number (SIN) to open a basic account.

## FCAC Administrative Monetary Penalties — Key Violations

| Violation | Maximum Penalty |
|-----------|----------------|
| Failure to provide required cost of borrowing disclosure | $10M |
| Charging a fee not disclosed in the fee disclosure document | $10M |
| Unsolicited credit limit increase without consent | $10M |
| Negative option billing for optional products | $10M |
| Failure to maintain a compliant complaint process | $10M |
| Failure to provide annual mortgage statement | $10M |
| Failure to refer customer to ECB | $10M |

FCAC publishes compliance reports naming institutions and the nature of violations. Reputational consequences often exceed the monetary penalty.
