# FCAC — Financial Consumer Agency of Canada

## Overview

The **Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC)** is the federal regulator responsible for protecting the rights and interests of consumers of financial products and services offered by **federally regulated financial institutions (FRFIs)** — primarily chartered banks.

FCAC enforces the consumer provisions of the **Bank Act**, **Insurance Companies Act**, **Trust and Loan Companies Act**, and **FCAC Act**. It does not regulate provincial credit unions, insurance brokers, or payday lenders — those fall under provincial jurisdiction.

**FCAC powers:** Investigate complaints → Issue compliance agreements → Name and shame (public compliance reports) → Impose administrative monetary penalties up to **$10 million per violation**

## Core Consumer Rights Under the Bank Act

### 1. Right to a Basic Bank Account
Every Canadian has the right to open a **basic bank account** at any federally regulated bank, subject to limited exceptions:

- Banks **cannot** require a minimum deposit or minimum balance
- Banks **cannot** require a credit check as a condition of opening a basic account
- Banks **must** open an account for any individual who presents valid government-issued ID and requests one
- **Exceptions** (bank may decline): Individual has been convicted of a financial crime in past 7 years; individual has made a false statement to the bank; individual has previously committed fraud against the bank

**What a basic account includes:** Ability to deposit cheques, make withdrawals, pay bills, and receive direct deposits. Banks are not required to offer credit or overdraft on a basic account.

### 2. Cheque Hold Periods
Banks may place holds on cheques, but federal regulations cap the maximum hold period:

| Cheque Type | Maximum Hold |
|-------------|-------------|
| Government of Canada cheques | Next business day |
| Cheques drawn on another account at the same branch | Next business day |
| Cheques drawn on a Canadian bank (≤ $1,500) | Next business day |
| Cheques drawn on a Canadian bank (> $1,500) | 7 business days |
| All other cheques | At bank's discretion (longer holds permitted) |

Banks must provide $100 of the deposited funds by the next business day regardless of any hold.

### 3. NSF (Non-Sufficient Funds) Fees
- FCAC published a **voluntary code** limiting NSF fees — major banks committed to maximum **$25 per NSF item** (reduced from previous levels)
- Banks must clearly disclose NSF fee amounts in their fee disclosure documents
- Banks must provide **advance notice** before implementing any new or increased fees (minimum 30 days)
- Customers must be notified of any fee changes through their statement or online banking

### 4. Right to Cancel Credit Products
- Customers may cancel a credit card within **30 days** of receiving it without penalty, even if they have used it, as long as the outstanding balance is paid
- Customers may cancel a fixed-rate loan and prepay without penalty if the loan is for personal use (subject to prepayment penalty provisions — see below)

## Cost of Borrowing — Disclosure Requirements

The **Cost of Borrowing Regulations** require banks to provide standardized disclosure before and after entering into a credit agreement:

### Credit Cards
**Before account opening (pre-contractual disclosure):**
- Annual interest rate (purchase, cash advance, balance transfer — separately)
- Annual fee
- Grace period (minimum days before interest accrues on purchases)
- Minimum payment formula
- Any balance protection or insurance product offered

**Monthly statement requirements:**
- Opening and closing balance
- Transactions listed with dates
- Interest charged (separately for purchases, cash advances, balance transfers)
- Minimum payment amount **and** the date by which it must be paid
- The time it would take to pay off the balance if only minimum payments are made
- The total interest cost if only minimum payments are made

**Minimum Payment Warning (mandatory since 2010):** Every statement must include a warning showing the time and total interest cost of paying only the minimum — a powerful disclosure requirement.

### Mortgages
**Before signing:**
- Total mortgage amount
- Amortization period
- Payment frequency and amount
- Interest rate and type (fixed/variable)
- Term
- **Prepayment privileges** — what the borrower is permitted to prepay without penalty
- **Prepayment penalty** — how it is calculated if the borrower exceeds privileges or breaks the mortgage

**After signing:** Annual mortgage statement showing remaining balance, interest paid, principal paid, and remaining amortization.

### Personal Loans and Lines of Credit
- Annual percentage rate (APR) or annual interest rate
- Total amount of credit
- Total cost of borrowing (if calculable)
- Payment schedule
- Any conditions under which the rate may change

## Mortgage Prepayment Rights

Under the **Bank Act**, federally regulated banks must:
- Permit borrowers to prepay at least **10% of the original principal** once per year without penalty (for residential mortgages with a fixed rate and term)
- Disclose the prepayment charge formula clearly in the mortgage agreement
- Provide a written prepayment charge estimate within **3 business days** of a request

### Prepayment Penalty Calculation Methods

**Interest Rate Differential (IRD) — most common for fixed-rate mortgages:**
Penalty = Remaining principal × (Contract rate − Current posted rate for equivalent remaining term) × Remaining months ÷ 12

**3 Months' Interest — often used for variable-rate or shorter remaining terms:**
Penalty = Remaining principal × Annual interest rate × 3 ÷ 12

Banks must use the **higher** of the two methods. The IRD can be significant when rates have fallen since the mortgage was originated.

**FCAC requirement:** Banks must provide a prepayment charge estimate within 3 business days if asked. Customers have a right to this information.

## Complaint Handling — Tiered Process

All federally regulated banks must have a **complaint handling process** that meets FCAC standards:

### Tier 1 — Bank's Internal Process
1. Customer contacts bank's front-line service (branch, phone, online)
2. If unresolved, escalation to bank's dedicated complaints department
3. Bank must acknowledge within **5 business days**
4. Bank must provide a final response within **56 days** (8 weeks) of receiving the complaint
5. If unresolved or customer is not satisfied → must refer customer to external body

### Tier 2 — External Complaints Body (ECB)
Banks must belong to one of two approved ECBs:
- **OBSI (Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments):** The dominant ECB for most major banks
- **ADR Chambers Banking Ombuds Office (ADRBO):** Alternative ECB used by some banks

ECBs provide free, independent dispute resolution. Their recommendations are not binding but banks comply in the vast majority of cases. Maximum award: **$350,000**.

### Tier 3 — FCAC
FCAC does not resolve individual disputes but investigates whether the bank followed its own complaint process and complied with the Bank Act consumer provisions. Consumers can file a complaint at **fcac-acfc.gc.ca**.

## Credit Card Specific Protections

| Protection | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Grace period | Minimum **21 days** interest-free on new purchases if previous balance paid in full |
| Over-limit transactions | Banks must obtain **express consent** before allowing over-limit transactions; must disclose any over-limit fee |
| Balance protection insurance | Disclosure of total cost required; opt-in consent (not negative option billing) |
| Unsolicited credit limit increases | **Prohibited** — bank must obtain express consent before increasing a credit limit |
| Summary of rights | Banks must provide a summary of credit card rights to every new cardholder |

## Plain Language and Accessibility Requirements

- All product disclosure documents must be written in **plain language** — clear, simple, and easy to understand
- Documents must be available in both **English and French** (bilingual institutions) — Bank Act requires French-language services in regions with significant Francophone populations and across Canada for national communications
- Electronic documents must meet accessibility standards — FCAC is aligning with WCAG 2.1 AA for digital disclosures
