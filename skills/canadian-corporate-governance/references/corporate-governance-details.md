# Corporate Governance Deep Reference

## Director Onboarding Checklist

When a new director joins the board, complete the following:

### Legal & Regulatory
- [ ] Director consents in writing to act as director (CBCA s.106(9)) — retain signed consent
- [ ] Confirm director eligibility: not bankrupt, not been found guilty of fraud in past 5 years, not a minor
- [ ] At least 25% of directors of a CBCA corporation must be resident Canadians (some exceptions for small boards)
- [ ] File notice of change of directors with Corporations Canada within 15 days (CBCA) or as required provincially
- [ ] Issue Director Indemnification Agreement (indemnifies director for good-faith acts within their authority)
- [ ] Arrange Directors' and Officers' (D&O) liability insurance — brief director on coverage
- [ ] Register director in the ISC Register if they qualify as an Individual with Significant Control

### Orientation
- [ ] Provide Articles of Incorporation, By-laws, and any Unanimous Shareholder Agreement
- [ ] Provide last 3 years of financial statements and most recent auditor's report
- [ ] Provide last 12 months of board meeting minutes
- [ ] Brief director on key contracts, litigation, regulatory matters, and pending transactions
- [ ] Introduce to management team and key advisors (legal counsel, auditors)
- [ ] Review and sign any required confidentiality/conflict of interest policies

### Ongoing
- [ ] Add director to conflict of interest declaration process (annual or upon arising)
- [ ] Include in D&O insurance renewal notifications
- [ ] Confirm attendance at upcoming board meetings and provide calendar

## Minute Book Audit Tool

Use this checklist to assess the completeness of a corporation's minute book:

### Organizational Documents
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation (or Certificate of Continuance if continued from another jurisdiction)
- [ ] Articles of Incorporation (and all Amendments)
- [ ] By-law No. 1 (General By-law) — adopted by directors and confirmed by shareholders
- [ ] Any special by-laws or resolutions amending by-laws
- [ ] Unanimous Shareholder Agreement (if any)

### Registers
- [ ] Register of Directors: all past and present directors with start/end dates
- [ ] Register of Officers: all current officers
- [ ] Register of Shareholders: all shareholders with current address and number/class of shares held
- [ ] Register of Transfers: record of all share transfers
- [ ] Register of Individuals with Significant Control (ISC Register): as required under CBCA 2023 amendments
- [ ] Register of Mortgages and Charges (if applicable)

### Meeting Records
- [ ] Minutes of all Annual Meetings of Shareholders (past 6 years minimum)
- [ ] Minutes of all Special Meetings of Shareholders
- [ ] Minutes of all Board Meetings (past 6 years minimum)
- [ ] Minutes of all Committee Meetings (audit, compensation, governance)
- [ ] Signed proxy forms for all meetings where proxies were solicited
- [ ] Written resolutions in lieu of meetings (signed by all required parties)

### Financial Records
- [ ] Audited financial statements (past 6 fiscal years)
- [ ] Management representation letters to auditors
- [ ] Reports to shareholders

### Common Deficiencies
- Directors never formally approved first by-law
- Share issuances never recorded in share register
- Post-incorporation organizational resolutions missing
- Minutes from early years lost or never prepared
- ISC register not established or not filed with Corporations Canada

## Annual Compliance Calendar — CBCA Corporation (December 31 Fiscal Year-End)

| Month | Action |
|-------|--------|
| January | Prepare board calendar for the year; confirm D&O insurance renewal |
| February | Prepare annual financial statements; brief audit committee |
| March | Annual audit completion; issue financial statements to shareholders |
| April | Annual Meeting of Shareholders (AGM) — within 6 months of FYE |
| April–May | File Annual Return with Corporations Canada (within 60 days of anniversary date) |
| June 15 | File/confirm ISC Register with Corporations Canada |
| July | Mid-year review of conflicts of interest |
| October | Board approves preliminary budget for next year |
| November–December | Board approves final budget; year-end tax planning; review and renew key contracts |
| Ongoing (within 15 days) | File notice of change for any director/officer changes; update ISC Register for any changes |

## Director Liability Exposure — Quick Reference

| Liability Area | Legislation | Cap | Defence |
|---------------|------------|-----|---------|
| Unpaid wages (up to 6 months) | CBCA s.119 / ESA | 6 months wages per employee | Due diligence (active oversight) |
| Unremitted CPP/EI/income tax | Income Tax Act s.227.1 | Full amount | Due diligence |
| Unremitted HST/GST | Excise Tax Act s.323 | Full amount | Due diligence (resign within 2 years) |
| Environmental cleanup | CEPA / provincial EA acts | Potentially unlimited | Reasonable care |
| Securities misrepresentation | Provincial securities acts | Potential civil liability | Reasonable investigation |
| Oppression remedy | CBCA s.241 | Court's discretion | Business judgment; procedural fairness |

**Due diligence defence (tax):** A director is not liable if they exercised the degree of care, diligence, and skill a reasonably prudent person would exercise in comparable circumstances to prevent the failure to remit. Courts look at: was the director actively engaged, did they enquire about remittances, were they relying on a specific competent person?

**Resignation timing:** For tax liability, a director must resign within **2 years** of the assessment to retain the option of raising the due diligence defence. A resigned director is still liable for failures that occurred while they were a director.
